What's new in today's update:

We've made the quiz smarter and more challenging by testing you in both directions for every topic — not just one way.

- Synonyms — You'll now sometimes be given a synonym and asked to find the original word, not just the other way around.

- Antonyms — Same idea: sometimes you get the antonym and have to name the word it belongs to.

- Collective Nouns — New "reverse" questions like "A bunch of ___ ?" alongside the usual "What is the collective noun for flowers?"

- Oxymorons — New fill-in-the-blank style: "Complete the oxymoron: Act ___" — so you're tested on the pair itself, not just its meaning.

- Multiple correct answers — For topics like collective nouns and synonyms, some questions now ask you to select ALL correct answers, since a word can have more than one valid synonym or collective noun. At least 1 in 5 questions will be this type.

Everything else (idioms, similes, proverbs, one-word substitutions) was already testing both directions — no change there.
